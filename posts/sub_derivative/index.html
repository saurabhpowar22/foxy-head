What is substantial derivative ??

Substantial derivative is the total change in property of a fluid particle

as it moves though SPACE

and TIME

As it transverses from initial point 1 to point 2 given by,

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{D\phi}{Dt} = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y} + w \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial z} \end{aligned} \]

but we know the bookish definition but what it really means ??

Lets simplify things a bit and understand through example,

Analogy to substantial derivative

Suppose we have a college student, whose name is Rohan. And we want to track rohan’s attitude change over a semester, how to we do that ?

first let’s assign a symbol to rohan’s attitude : $\phi$

Attitude at start of semester : $\phi_1$
Attitude at end of semester : $\phi_2$

what are the things that will change rohan’s attitude ?, peeking into his life we can see few things that will affect him over time.

  1. Firstly our boy rohan got a new girlfriend (wooo hooooo !!!!!!)
  2. Hanging out with buddies (he believes in apes strong together)
  3. This semester rohan has to deal with few hard subjects
  4. Attitude change due to time itself as rohan becomes mature

Now how to figure out total change in attitude ?
Simply adding each factor up !

How to represent each factor in mathematical terms

We have neat little trick of representing each factor with : \[ (\text{Rate of attitude change}) \times (\text{Things done overall}) \]

For example attitude change due to girlfriend can be represented by,

rate of attitude change due to girlfriend can be given by,

\[ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial(\text{GF})} \]

and multiplying it with $(\text{shenanigans done with GF})$ gives us attitude change due to girlfriend

\[ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial(\text{GF})} \times (\text{shenanigans done with GF}) \]

Lets take situation where rohan’s girlfriend breaks up with him, suddenly taking rohan from one love to no love. It will definitely have impact on rohan’s attitude and behaviour

Also the way she breaks up with him will also impact “rate of attitude change”

Similarly it can be done for other factors.

Total attitude change of rohan over semester

Now we are at point where we can add each factor and estimate rohan’s attitude change as, $(\Delta \phi) = \phi_2 - \phi_1$

\[ \begin{aligned} \phi_1 - \phi_2 &= \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial (\text{GF})} (\text{shenanigans done with GF})\newline & + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial (\text{buddies})} (\text{Hanging our with buddies})\newline & + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial (\text{academics pressure})} (\text{pressure built up})\newline & + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial (\text{Time})} (\text{Total time passed})\newline \end{aligned} \]

Hopefully Rohan will be okay by the end of the semester ;)

Connecting substantial derivative idea to fluid mechanics

For a fluid particle moving from point 1 to point 2 in flow, it is moving with respect space (x,y,z directions) and also in time (as particle can’t travel instantaneously)

so we have below factors changing for fluid particle :

  • x-direction
  • y-direction
  • z-direction
  • t (time)

Now we can track fluid property changes with respect to these factors.

Let’s take temperature (T) of particle as it moves from point 1 to point 2

  • temperature at point 1 : $T_1$
  • temperature at point 2 : $T_2$

Change due to x-direction movement

\[ \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} (x_2 - x_1) \]

Here,

  • $\frac{\partial T}{\partial x}$ gives us the rate of temperature change due to transversing in x-direction &
  • $(x_2 - x_1)$ tells us how much particle has travelled in x-direction.

Similarly it can be done for other factors.

Change due to y-direction movement

\[ \frac{\partial T}{\partial y} (y_2 - y_1) \]

Change due to z-direction movement

\[ \frac{\partial T}{\partial z} (z_2 - z_1) \]

Change due to time

\[ \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} (t_2 - t_1) \]

Total Change while moving from point 1 to point 2

Similar to our example where we added up each factor, here also we are going to add each factor to find out total change,

\[ \begin{aligned} (T_2 - T_1) &= \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} (x_2 - x_1) \newline & + \frac{\partial T}{\partial y} (y_2 - y_1)\newline & + \frac{\partial T}{\partial z} (z_2 - z_1)\newline & + \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} (t_2 - t_1) \end{aligned} \]

Now if we wanted to find out this total change with accordance to time we can just divide $(T_2 - T_1)$ by $(t_2 - t_1)$

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \frac{(T_2 - T_1)}{(t_2 - t_1)} &= \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} \frac{(x_2 - x_1)}{(t_2 - t_1)} + \frac{\partial T}{\partial y} \frac{(y_2 - y_1)}{(t_2 - t_1)} \newline &+ \frac{\partial T}{\partial z} \frac{(z_2 - z_1)}{(t_2 - t_1)} + \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} \end{aligned} \end{equation} \]

In above equation(1) we can see the meaning of substantial derivative, change in fluid property due all the factors as it moves through space

Suppose we wanted to know change in temperature at particular instance, what can we do ?

we can shrink our time difference $t_2 - t_1$ to a very small value by putting it in limit, as $t_2$ approaches very close to $t_1$

\[ \lim_{t_2 \to t_1} \frac{T_2 - T_1}{t_2 - t_1} = \frac{DT}{Dt} \]

similarly we have velocity terms from equation(1),

  • x velocity ($u$) \[ \lim_{t_2 \to t_1} \frac{x_2 - x_1}{t_2 - t_1} = u \]

  • y velocity ($v$) \[ \lim_{t_2 \to t_1} \frac{y_2 - y_1}{t_2 - t_1} = v \]

  • z velocity ($w$) \[ \lim_{t_2 \to t_1} \frac{z_2 - z_1}{t_2 - t_1} = w \]

putting everything in equation(1) we get,

\[ \frac{DT}{Dt} = \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial T}{\partial y} + w \frac{\partial T}{\partial z} \tag{2} \]

equation(2) represents substantial (Total) derivative for temperature, but we can find substantial derivative any fluid property not just temperature

Let $\phi$ be general fluid property, hence we can generalize our equation(2) as

\[ \frac{D\phi}{Dt} = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y} + w \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial z} \tag{3} \]

which can be compactly written in vector form as,

\[ \frac{D\phi}{Dt} = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} + (\vec{V} \cdot \nabla \phi) \tag{4} \]

$\vec{V}$ is a velocity vector with its components as $\vec{V} = [u,v,w]$

We can see 2 terms in equation(4)

  • Local derivative : Changes due to time

\[ \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} \]

  • Convective derivative : Changes due to movement of particle

\[ (\vec{V} \cdot \nabla \phi) \]

Equation(4) form can be used for any co-ordinate system such as spherical, cylindrical etc.
